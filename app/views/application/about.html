About

&{'app.name'} is a platform for designing models of interaction among multiple parties. Such models are used by psychologists and economists to investigate a wide range of phenomena, including conflict and cooperation within and between groups, individual judgment & decision making, and testing models of evolutionary dynamics, trust and negotiations, and public policy.


Survey platforms currently lack this vital feature. in &{'app.name'} we have sought to fill this gap. With a simple user interface we have made it possible to design an experiment in no time, and even more so, run as many participants as needed in very short time. All of this is made possible at a significant reduced price.


Let’s be specific. Many popular tools that are used to test human interactions are based on game theory. Pick the Ultimatum Game for example. The game has two players, one rule, and two possible outcomes. One player (proposer) divides a pool of resources and allocates a fraction to the second player (responder). The responder decides whether to accept or reject the offered share. Acceptance leads to both players keeping their shares while rejecting means both players lose their share.


Two variables emerge from a one-time-interaction version of the game: the division of resources by the proposer and the reaction of the responder. What is interesting here is whether and how the two players behave. Behavioral in-lab studies show people on average reject offers below 30% of the initial common pool. Whether this pattern upholds in online setting is itself an interesting questions.

Could it be that people are sensitive to how the roles (proposer vs. responder) are assigned? We can test that in &{'app.name'}. Could it be that people choose to cooperate with certain races, nationalities,, genders, age groups etc? We can simply cutomize player profiles on &{'app.name'} to test these explanations.
